String vs Int: The battle for Palindrome Plains

Oct 31st, 2010 Comments

I have a homework for the University in which I have to say if a number is a palindrome or not. So I was wondering which method is going to be faster? Reading it as a string and checking it character by character or reading it as a int and comparing it to it’s reverse?  INT Pros: Faster checking Cons: Had to generate the reverse of it which included using the % (mod) operator which we all know is time consuming STRING Pros: Only had to check the first letters of a number in the case the number wasn’t palindrome. Cons: Had to use strlen to get the length of the string. So I had written some simple scripts pretty fast and I was amazed to see that on a random dataset the string method is somewhere between 10-20% faster. I made an archive with all the scripts and the random number generator, you can get it here. I am pretty sure that I can make a way in which I can find the length of the string from reading it, without reading character by character. What do you say?

Distributed CEO

Mar 28th, 2010 Comments

Via ganceanu.com Distributed CEO via Silviu Gănceanu on 3/27/10My 3 weeks of being CEO will expire on Monday and I will pass the CEO hat to Mircea. It’s been a crazy and fun period for us! I had the chance to manage one “intimate” beta launch and one private beta launch. It all ended up with an awesome private beta party this Friday — 55 beers and 1 bottle of whiskey is what we accomplished together with our Bootup Labs friends. Since many people are asking us who’s the CEO in our team I am going to tell you the story behind our “system”. The quick answer is this: at Summify we have a distributed CEO - each of us wears the CEO hat for 3 weeks and we rotate. When we started working at Balaur.ro we didn’t have a CEO: we we’re managing our operations all together, we were going to meetings together, etc. We’ve soon realized that this is inefficient. Luckily we’ve attended one of Radu Georgescu’s talks about entrepreneurship. The talk was awesome: he made a comparison between Poker and Business :) One of things that he pointed out was the conflict of interests between the different layers in a company: shareholders, board of directors, CEO and employees. When you’re running your own startup you have a hat for each of these layers: you are a shareholder and a director, sometimes you act as the manager but most of the time your just doing whatever needs to be done to push the company just another inch forward. At that talk we realized that we have to play a CEO game of some sort. That someone has to assume this role, manage day to day operations and make things happen. Since we were all interested in being a CEO we came up with a system so that each of us gets to run the company for a fixed period of time. At the end of each week during this period the other two guys in the team magically transform from employees into shareholders and judge the CEO based on results. That’s how the conflict of interests gets born and this is pure health for the company: it creates an isolated moment when valuable feedback is brought on the table, the CEO gets his credit or some kicks in the nuts :) For us it went great so far: we are constantly making progress and each of us is improving his CEO’s skills based on the feedback he gets from the shareholders. This trick enabled us to run better our startup and get a lot more things done. We’re constantly looking to improve our ways to run our company so if you have any suggestion or comment I will be happy to hear from you. Posted via email from Alexandru’s posterous | Comment »

Balaur.ro

Nov 5th, 2009 Comments

One of the best romanian job search engines Balaur.ro has just added a new quite unique functionality. You now can upload your CV and it will automatically search for jobs based on your CV. The phrase matching in the CV was pretty amazing.. but from what I see their job matching functionality still has a little to work on.. it matched “Reasearch and Development” with “department manager research and development” but hey the feature is only in Beta. Currently you can upload a CV only in .doc, .pdf or .txt format, but adding support for open formats like .odt would probably be more appreciated by the open-source comunity. Looking at their search engine I see that there are still things to work on.. like for example if I searched for “Virus Analyst” and came up with a result on target and the rest… well let’s say that they we’re not related to viruses at least anyway it was way better than their main competitors who gave me the results for any job containing virus and analist in it. From what I have seen in their presentation at Geek Meet they use Xapian Library to do the searches, my word of advice would be to implement something more specific if they want to be ahead of the game! It’s not features like uploading CV’s to find you the right job that makes a search engine good, it’s the quality of their results! Work more on improving it than on writing features for it, you did not see Google working on a “new aproach to mail” before they began the biggest search engine, did you?  And try indexing more sites..(your site did not find some of the offers that all the other search engines did) in this business information is everythng!
